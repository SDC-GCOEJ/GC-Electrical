About Us

Preamble:

The department of Electrical Engineering was established on 30th May 1996 as institute level laboratory in electrical engineering. Since its commencement, the primary objective of the department has been to impart quality education, training and research at the undergraduate and postgraduate in various areas of Electrical Engineering with broad emphasis on design aspects of electrical machines and power systems.

The Government of Maharashtra started under graduate course in electrical engineering with 60 intake capacity in the year 2007-08 affiliated to North Maharashtra University. Today departmental laboratories are equipped with all practical labs with modern equipments. More than 200 students are seeking professional education in the department under the supervision and guidance of all the faculties and guest faculties.

The Department has always been on a high growth path and has experienced and dedicated faculty with a strong commitment to engineering education. The department aims at initiating post graduate and Ph.d programmes within two years.To keep pace with the current technological trends, all the undergraduate and postgraduates curricula have been upgraded by NMU Jalgaon regularly.

The major areas of faculty expertise of the department include Basic Electrical and Electronics, Power Systems, Electrical machines, Control Systems, Digital Signal Processing, Embedded Systems, Instrumentation, Power Electronics, Electrical Measurement Systems, Switchgear and Protection Systems, Energy audit and Conservation Systems etc. The department focuses on preparing its students to meet new business challenges and making them aware of the recent advances in the technological field by emphasizing on:
A strong technical training in modern electrical technology
Skills in analyzing and design of power and control systems
Electrical Power generation, transmission and distribution management
Latest trends in electrical measurement and machine design
Advanced Embedded and DSP ,computer networking
Practical skills in electrical circuit and network design. In addition to the academic achievements, the students have been demonstrating skills in various extracurricular activities including paper Presentations, sports, drama, etc. They have been conducting various activities like personality development programmes, group discussions, workshops, etc. Dr. A.R. Phadke is working as Head of the Electrical Engineering department who is having 26 years teaching experience which includes 15 years post graduate teaching experience, and 20 years of experience as member of Board of studies, member of Faculty in engineering of various universities like Pune, Dr.BAMU Aurangabad , DSB/DFB/ Academic cell member in autonomous G.E.C., Aurangabad.

Programmes Educational Objectives

To prepare students to compete for a successful career in Electrical Engineering profession through global education standards
To enable the students to aptly apply their acquired knowledge in basic sciences and mathematics in solving Electrical ,Electronics and Communication, Computer Engineering etc core and interdisciplinary engineering problems
To produce skillful graduates to analyze, design and develop a system/component/ process for the required needs under the realistic constraints
To train the students to approach ethically any multidisciplinary engineering challenges with economic, environmental and social contexts
To create an awareness among the students about the need for lifelong learning to succeed in their professional career as Electrical Engineers

Programmes Outcomes

Graduates will demonstrate knowledge of mathematics, science and engineering Graduates will demonstrate the ability to identify, formulate and solve engineering problems
Graduate will demonstrate the ability to design and conduct experiments, analyze and interpret data
Graduates will demonstrate the ability to design a system, component or process as per needs and specifications
Graduates will demonstrate the ability to visualize and work on laboratory and multi-disciplinary tasks
